/03 · the elephant in the room

Yes, we know exactly what you're thinking.

Auto-applying to jobs sounds like the kind of thing that gets your account banned, your resume blacklisted, or both. We thought hard about each of those. Here's where we landed.

CONCERN · 01

"Won't LinkedIn ban me?"

JobCrawler runs inside your Chrome session — same cookies, same fingerprint, same IP as when you browse normally. No headless browser, no proxy, no API abuse. From LinkedIn's side it looks like a logged-in human clicking through Easy Apply slowly. We've run 1.4M applications across 8,000 accounts. Zero bans.

0 bans across 1.4M applications
CONCERN · 02

"What if it applies to a job I'd hate?"

Set a minimum score (default 75). Set a hard blocklist of companies. Set a "review queue" mode where it surfaces matches and you tap to apply. Defaults are conservative on purpose — most users start in review mode for the first 24 hours, then graduate to autopilot once they trust the scoring.

review-mode for the first 24h, by default
CONCERN · 03

"Where does my data go?"

Your resume and profile sit on our cloud (encrypted, India region). Job descriptions are sent to our scoring API, which forwards them to the LLM with your profile redacted of PII. We never store LinkedIn cookies, never see your password, and you can wipe everything with one button. Source code for the extension is public.

/06 · faq

Real questions.
Honest answers.

Didn't find yours? [email protected]

Will LinkedIn detect this and ban my account? +

Short answer: no. Long answer: JobCrawler runs inside your real Chrome session — it's a Manifest V3 extension, not a bot. There's no automated browser, no scraped API, no IP rotation. From LinkedIn's perspective, it's you, clicking buttons. We add randomized delays (40s–4min between applications) so the cadence looks human. Across 1.4M applications and 8,000+ accounts, we've never had a single ban. If it ever happens, we refund your year and help you appeal.

What if it applies to a job I'd never want? +

You set the minimum match score (we suggest 75/100 for ML and backend roles). You set a company blocklist. You can require a manual tap before any apply — that's "review mode" and we recommend it for the first 24 hours so you can calibrate. The LLM also has hard rules: it won't apply if your stated minimum salary clearly exceeds the JD band, or if seniority is wildly off (no SDE-1 jobs for staff engineers).

Does it answer those custom screening questions? +

Yes — for the easy ones (years of experience, notice period, current CTC, expected CTC, work auth) it pulls from your profile. For long-form questions ("Why this role?"), the default behavior is to flag for review and ping you. You can also save canned answers per role family. We will never fabricate work experience or credentials.

Where is my resume stored? +

Encrypted at rest in AWS Mumbai (ap-south-1). It's only decrypted in-memory when the extension uploads it to LinkedIn or when scoring needs to read your skills. We don't sell data. We don't share with recruiters. There's a "delete everything" button in Settings that takes 5 seconds and is irreversible.

Do you support non-Easy-Apply jobs? +

Not yet. Easy Apply covers ~70% of relevant LinkedIn postings and lets us guarantee submission integrity. External-redirect jobs (Greenhouse, Lever, company portals) are on the roadmap for Q3 2026 — they require per-ATS adapters and a different consent flow.

Can I pause or stop a run mid-way? +

One click. Or close the LinkedIn tab. Or hit Cmd/Ctrl + period. Or just put your laptop to sleep. Any of those — and the next run picks up cleanly from where the queue left off.

Why ₹499 and not free? +

The LLM scoring API isn't free for us — every job-resume pair is roughly ₹0.40 in inference. At 300 jobs/week per active user that's already ~₹150/month before infra and support. ₹499 keeps us small, sustainable, and uninterested in selling your data. If you land a job, you'll cancel — and we're fine with that.
